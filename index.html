The Australian Aviation Industry and Melbourne from 2019 to June 2021



Background Infomation

The Australian aviation industry has been effected drastically by the effects of COVID-19, and the substantial knock-on effects from extended lockdowns, international border closures and outbreaks. This has lead to the collaspe of one of Australia's two major airlines, Virgin Australia, along with its counterpart budget airline, TigerAir Australia. Currently, the aviation industry within Australia is dominated by Qantas, and its low-cost carrier Jetstar, however, they may soon be facing competition from regional operator Rex, or a revival of Virgin Australia under a new name.

This visualisation aims to show the state of the Australian aviation industry from 2019 to June 2021, focusing on the city of Melbourne, since it arguebly has been affected the most, with over 200 days in lockdown since the begining of the pandemic.

A Overview Australia's Commericial Aviation Industry

Qantas has been the dominant carrier in Australia since it was founded in 1920. It currently operates the low-cost carrier Jetstar and is part of the OneWorld group joining airlines such as American Airways and Cathay Pacific.
